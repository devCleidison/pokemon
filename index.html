Generations

Generation I

The origin of Pokémon is in two games called Poketto Monsutā Midori, better known as Pokémon Green, and Poketto Monsuttā Aka, known in the west as Pokémon Red, released in 1996, in Japan. Pokémon Green was never released outside of Japan, being released only Pokémon Red Version and Blue Version. Pokémon Blue was also released in Japan a few months after the first two were released. Due to the success of these games, which became some of the best-selling games in Japan in 1996,[13] a different version was produced, called Pokémon Yellow: Special Pikachu Edition; had as main Pokémon a Pikachu that was always following the player out of his Poké Ball. Pokémon Red & Blue has sold over ten million copies in the West and Pokémon Yellow has sold over five million copies alone.

Generation II

As it gained more and more fans worldwide, the creators decided to make a sequel to the first games. This generated in two new versions, which brought an adventure different from the previous ones, located in a Region next to the stage of the first games, 100 new Pokémon, totaling 251, new features, among others. Known as Pokémon Gold & Silver, they served as the basis for the mechanism of the following ones, presenting the distinction between male and female Pokémon, enabling the crossing of Pokémon, a concept often used for exchanges between players, using time within the game, differentiating the day of the night and many other news for players.

Generation III

In March 2003, the first third-generation games were released, better known to gamers as the Advance generation, as their original RPGs were made for the Game Boy Advance handheld: Pokémon Ruby & Sapphire, which surpassed 3.1 million units sold, thus being the most commercialized in Japan in 2002[16] brought 135 new Pokémon, but it was impossible to transfer Pokémon from previous generations to the games, as their training in Pokémon Ruby & Sapphire was based on another system, that of Effort Values ​​(effort values), points that, when well calculated and distributed to the Pokémon, made it stronger than others trained without this method. In order to get some of the older Pokémon, Pokémon Ruby & Sapphire would link up with other third-gen games, most notably Pokémon Colosseum, one of that generation's platform-type games.

Generation IV

In September 2006, the first two versions of original RPGs of fourth generation Pokémon, made for the Nintendo DS, were released in Japan. Pokémon Diamond & Pearl introduced 106 new Pokémon, totaling 493, new functions, such as a change in the attacks part, dividing them into three types (Physical, which uses the Attack value; Special, which uses the Special Attack value and Other, which uses any other value), the return of time changes between day and night, last used in Pokémon Gold & Silver, including the ability to connect online through Nintendo Wi-Fi Connection, where players from anywhere on the world could battle and trade Pokémon with various other players on the planet. Also introduced in Nintendo DS the use of a headset that allowed conversations between players who would face off on the Nintendo WFC. Pokémon Diamond & Pearl sold over 1.58 million copies in its first four days in Japan[21] and were also the country's best-selling games for the entire year of 2006.[22] In the United States Pokémon Diamond & Pearl sold more than one million copies in the first week of sales[23] and more than ten worldwide.

Generation V

The names of the 5th generation versions are: Pokémon Black and Pokémon White! The revelation was made by the official page of the little monsters. More details will come on the 15th, but the information released shows that the launch will take place in the Japanese fall. As expected, the first big highlight is the 3D graphics that are better developed compared to previous versions. Then we have the new battle screen, as shown in the image with Zorua and Zoroark, the first little monsters of the new generation. The Pokémon Center was upgraded and now has a sales counter, possibly the result of a merger with the Pokémon Marketplace. Notice, in the same image, that an indication of which character is speaking is now shown, as if it were a comic book balloon.

Generation VI

New Pokémon X & Y games are announced for October 2013 now for a new platform, Nintendo 3DS, in the Kalos region. The three new starter Pokémon are Fennekin (Fire), Chespin (Grass) and Froakie (Water) and the legendary Pokémon Xerneas (Pokémon X) and Yveltal (Pokémon Y) with a new 3D gaming experience. This was the game that most surprised the entire franchise with many new features, starting with the one that most surprised: The Mega Evolutions, which are evolved versions of some Pokémon. It is a temporary evolution, so it only happens during a battle if the Pokémon holds its respective Mega Stone (the item that makes it mega evolve). Already mega evolved Pokémon can have a change in Status, Abilities and even the type of Pokémon! It's important to remember that only one Pokemon can Mega Evolve per battle! Another big news was the addition of the Fairy type that has new Pokémon and even some old ones that had this type added or replaced by the old one, such as Togekiss, which was Normal/Flying type and turned into Fairy/Flying type! This type was created in order to balance the game from the Competitive side that in the past generation was dominated by Dragons since Fairy has advantage and immunity to Dragons.

Generation VII

During a Nintendo Direct presentation on February 26, 2016, two new Pokémon titles were announced: Pokémon Sun and Pokémon Moon. The First Games of the seventh generation of Pokémon, slated for release in December 2016 but officially released in November of the same year.[26] The games will be compatible with other Nintendo 3DS titles, including Pokémon X and Y; Pokémon Omega Ruby and Alpha Sapphire; and the Virtual Console of re-releases of Pokemon Red, Blue and Yellow.

Generation VIII

On February 27, 2019, the franchise's first games on the nintendo switch[27], Pokémon Sword and Pokémon Shield were announced. The initial three pokemons revealed in the trailer are Scorbunny, fire, Sobble, water, and Grookey, grass. The legendary packs are Zacian, in the Sword version, and Zamazenta, in the Shield version. On a direct nintendo, it was revealed that not all of the Pokémon were going to be in the game, which made many fans angry, which caused #BringBackNationalDex (Bring Back National Dex), or #BBND to be created. On June 3, 2020, the DLCs [28] Pokémon Isle of Armor and Pokémon Crown of Tundra were announced.

About

The anime shows Ash Ketchum and his Pikachu on their quest to become a Pokémon master. His friends Brock and Misty accompany him for a large part of this journey. Other protagonists include Tracey, May and Max, Dawn, Iris and Cilan, Serena, Clemont and Bonnie. Throughout the series there is the presence of Team Rocket. Its three members that appear the most are Jessie, James and Meowth, who aim to steal Pokémon, especially Ash's Pikachu. Ash goes through various regions along his journey, such as Kanto, Johto, Hoenn, Sinnoh, Unova, Kalos and currently Alola. And so Ash and his friends explore this wonderful Pokémon world full of adventures and new species of Pokémon.
